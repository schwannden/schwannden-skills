# Father Anselm Grun — Leadership Philosophy

Benedictine monk, depth psychologist, and manager of 300 employees across 20 enterprises at Munsterschwarzach Abbey. Author of 300+ books (15M+ copies). Key works: "Menschen fuhren - Leben wecken", "Lead Like a Monk", "The Manager and the Monk".

## Core Principles

### 1. Self-Knowledge Before Leading Others

"Um andere gut zu fuhren, muss ich mich zuerst selbst fuhren." (To lead others well, I must first lead myself.)

The leader is "a person who has made peace with himself and is able to awaken liveliness and joy in his coworkers." Leading through personality is more important than any methods and strategies. Human maturity, humility, devotion, appreciation, and the right measure (discretio) are the best prerequisites for leadership.

**Practice:** Before a performance review, examine your own emotional state. Are you frustrated? Disappointed? Anxious? Name it. Deal with your own inner weather first so it does not leak into the conversation.

### 2. Feedback: Honest Yet Compassionate

- **Start from appreciation, not deficiency.** Meditate on what each person is passionate about, what they enjoy, where they could develop.
- **Speak from the heart.** Distinguish between "reden" (explaining/arguing) and "sprechen" (true speaking from within). People feel the difference between a template and genuine seeing.
- **Use discretio (discernment/measure).** Adapt your approach to each individual. Be "cautious and thoughtful" with "discretion and limits." Do not be "turbulentus" (unquiet, stormy).
- **Lift people up.** "Fuhren heisst auch, Menschen aufzurichten, sodass sie abends aufrecht nach Hause gehen." (Leading means lifting people up so they leave work standing tall.) Every feedback conversation should leave the person with more dignity, not less.

### 3. Vulnerability, Humility, and Authenticity

"Demut bezieht sich immer auf Selbsterkenntnis und Dankbarkeit." (Humility always relates to self-knowledge and gratitude.)

Humility means being ready to look at your own shadow. It means having correct self-estimation: neither inflated nor deflated. Leaders cannot inspire trust if they do not trust themselves.

**Benedict's Steps of Humility relevant to leadership:**
- Love not one's own will — prioritize mission over ego
- Be transparent — confess weaknesses honestly
- Have correct self-estimation
- Control one's tongue — practice restraint
- Speak clearly and plainly

### 4. Shadow Work and Projection (Jungian)

> "Wer sich selbst nicht kennt, der projiziert all die verdrangten Seiten auf seine Mitarbeiter."
> (Anyone who does not know themselves projects all their repressed sides onto their employees.)

The leader who has not examined their own shadow will unconsciously project onto their team. They will see laziness where there is a different working style. They will see insubordination where there is healthy disagreement.

**Practice:** Before writing a performance review, ask: "Is this criticism actually about them, or about something in me?" If an engineer's style irritates you, the irritation is information about you as much as about them.

### 5. Conflict and Difficult Conversations

- **Do not be "turbulentus."** Bring stillness, not storm, to conflict.
- **"Ausculta" (Listen).** Before correcting, judging, or deciding — listen deeply. Not just to words, but to the person behind the words.
- **Correction with discretion.** Proportionate, private, motivated by love. The goal is restoration, never humiliation.

### 6. Leading From the Soul

The leader is not an evaluator who ranks people, but an awakener who helps dormant potential come alive.

"Fuhren heisst, Leben in den Mitarbeitenden zu wecken." (Leading means awakening life in employees.)

- See the whole person — passions, fears, spiritual needs, creative potential
- Draw out, do not impose — help the person discover their own path
- Create environments of trust: "a sanctuary where a group of people mobilize their spiritual resources, ask relevant questions, love, trust, and respect one another"

### 7. The Meditation Practice for Leaders

Before each review or 1:1, sit quietly and reflect on the person: What are they passionate about? What do they enjoy? Where could they develop? Where have you seen them at their best? Enter the conversation from that place of attentive care.

### 8. Values-Based Leadership Qualities (from Benedict's Rule)

- Human maturity — emotional stability and self-awareness
- Humility — correct self-estimation and readiness to face one's shadow
- Justice — fairness in all dealings
- Decisiveness combined with restraint
- Wisdom — the capacity for discretio
- Fatherly/motherly care — strengthen people's backbone, not create dependency
